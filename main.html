Summary

Doctor Strange follows the story of brilliant but arrogant neurosurgeon Dr. Stephen Strange, whose career is shattered after a devastating car accident severely damages his hands. Desperate for a cure, he travels to Kamar-Taj, where he learns the mystical arts under the guidance of the Ancient One. As he masters powerful magic and discovers hidden dimensions beyond reality, Strange is drawn into a battle against the rogue sorcerer Kaecilius, who seeks to unleash the dark entity Dormammu upon Earth. Using his intelligence, courage, and the mystical Time Stone, Strange outwits Dormammu by trapping him in a time loop, forcing the cosmic being to retreat. By the end of the film, Strange embraces his new role as the protector of Earth, becoming the Sorcerer Supreme's most capable defender against supernatural threats.

Actor

Benedict Cumberbatch portrays Dr. Stephen Strange in the Marvel Cinematic Universe, bringing depth and charisma to the character through his compelling performance. His portrayal captures Strange's transformation from a brilliant yet arrogant neurosurgeon to a humble and powerful Master of the Mystic Arts, balancing intelligence, wit, and emotional growth. Cumberbatch's expressive acting, convincing American accent, and commanding screen presence made the character one of Marvel's most recognizable heroes. He first appeared as Doctor Strange in the 2016 film and has since reprised the role in several Marvel productions, including *Thor: Ragnarok*, *Avengers: Infinity War*, *Avengers: Endgame*, *Spider-Man: No Way Home*, and *Doctor Strange in the Multiverse of Madness*, becoming a central figure in the Marvel Cinematic Universe.
